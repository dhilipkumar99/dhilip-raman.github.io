Hi, I’m Dhilip Raman

I am a driven life-long learner of the biological sciences, and particularly neurobiology, with recent experience designing and conducting experiments to test virally modified cell transplants in the treatment of age-related macular degeneration (AMD). I also enjoy prototyping research aid tools in Python and R and reading research papers to stay informed on the exciting, state-of-the-art developments of new treatment methods. Check out some of my prototyped research tools, as well as the summaries of my previous research experiences, below.

My Resume

Age-Related Macular Degeneration Lab at Amsterdam University Medical Center

Assistant Clinical Research Coordinator (Aug 2023 – Present)

  • Ran multiple meta-analyses of genome-wide association studies (GWAS) of age-related macular degeneration (AMD) to identify causal genes for AMD
  • Designed and conducted experiments to implant cells with viral knockdown of ARMS2 and HTRA1 gene repressors in mice and test behavioral changes and effects on neuron pathway activation
  • Currently demonstrating that viral knockdown of ARMS2/HTRA1 repressors produces the AMD phenotype in mice
  • Assembled timelines and milestones for upcoming research projects by working cross-functionally with scientist and non-scientist teams
  • Managed goals and schedule of ongoing research studies while assisting with lab work

Center for Neurogenomics and Clinical Research (CNCR)

Research Assistant, Complex Trait Genetics (Nov 2022 – Jun 2023)

  • Investigated disease traits through functional annotation of multiple GWAS, using FUMA, to identify risk SNPs
  • Linked disease traits to risk genes through correlation of risk SNPs to genes, using both MAGMA and LD score regression methods
  • Identified cell types associated with disease traits using three cell type-association tools: FUMA’s Cell Type tool, CELLECT, and SC-linker
  • Developed a software tool in R to quickly access gene and protein profiles from fuma.ctglab.nl, in order to find causal genes of interest from disorders with similar phenotypes
  • Cultivated Parkinson’s-derived patient iPSCs and treated iPSC samples with viral vector containing truncated SNCA to identify the reaction of caspase-1 proteasomes in the modified SNCA environment
  • Demonstrated that in the modified SNCA environment, caspase-1 proteasomes are partially diverted from cleaving normal SNCA, resulting in lower alpha-synuclein levels
  • Thesis Paper: https://github.com/dhilipraman/Minimal-Overlap-Observed-in-Cell-Types-Associated-With-Parkinsons-Disease-GWAS

Amsterdam University Medical Center (AUMC)

Research Assistant, VUmc (Dec 2021 – Jul 2022)

  • Perform patient surveys and blood draws for Amsterdam UMC patients in the IMD and IMD-celecoxib trials
  • Organize clinical data for IMD and IMD-celecoxib trials from ~350 patients
  • Investigate coincidence of immunological and metabolic blood measures with energy-related depression symptoms to define immuno-metabolic depression (IMD) in IMD and IMD-celecoxib trials
  • Validate results by analyzing coincidence of these measures in the ongoing longitudinal NESDA study (Netherlands Study of Anxiety and Depression)
  • Ran regression analyses of the 13-year NESDA study of major depressive disorder (MDD) to help identify hallmarks of immuno-metabolic depression (IMD), a subset of MDD

  • Demonstrated significant improvement in depression symptoms for our patient group using a treatment of celecoxib, an anti-inflammation medication, coupled with cognitive behavioral therapy (CBT)
  • Thesis Paper: https://github.com/dhilipraman/Exploring-Definitions-for-Immuno-Metabolic-Subtype-of-Major-Depressive-Disorder

My Prototyped Research Tools

Elegans.ai: A machine learning algorithm using a Mask R-CNN segmentation model to trace C. elegans worms in fluorescent image data. Currently working with a group to rework this AI tool for tracing cell images.

Patient's Medical Glossary: A database of medical terms explained for laypeople; this resource has been provided to 23 nursing homes and clinics in San Diego to help assist in their medical outreach efforts.

GWAS Explorer: A research aid tool used to compare gene and protein profiles for similar medical disorders. Used to identify causal genes and proteins of interest for disorders with similar phenotypic traits, such as Parkinson's Disorder and Lewy Body Dementia.
